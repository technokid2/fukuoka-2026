City base at Royal Park Canvas Nakasu (Days 1–4 & 8–12), then move to private-onsen ryokans during the Kyushu interior loop (Days 5–7). Smooth circular route starting and ending near Fukuoka Airport.

Kurokawa Onsen — Gekkoju or Oku no Yu Ryokan

📍 Kurokawa Onsen Village, Minamioguni-machi, Kumamoto

Best accessed by rental car (2.5 hrs from Fukuoka).

Yufuin — Yufuin Gettouan Villa or Sanso Murata

📍 Yufuin Onsen, Yufu City, Oita Prefecture

Yufuin Gettouan: private villa-style rooms with outdoor onsen baths looking directly at Mt. Yufu. Romantic, surrounded by Japanese gardens. Sanso Murata: premium ryokan with exceptional kaiseki cuisine and private cypress baths. Both walkable from Lake Kinrin. 2 night stay to enjoy the town fully morning and evening.

⭐ Google 4.7/5 — “Waking up and stepping into the outdoor private bath with Mt. Yufu right there was the single most beautiful morning of our Japan trip. Absolutely magical.”

SGD 380–640/night2 nights (Days 6–7). Dinner + breakfast included.

💡 Booking priority: Ryokan rooms with private open-air onsen sell out months ahead for Aug/Sep. Book Kurokawa and Yufuin by May/June at the latest via Booking.com, Jalan, or Ikyu. Royal Park Canvas can be booked closer to the date.

💡 Essential Tips for This Trip

📋 Get IDP Before Departure

Apply for an International Driving Permit from Singapore’s Automobile Association before departure. Required alongside your Singapore licence for Japan car rental. Processing takes ~2 weeks. Essential for Days 4–8.

🔥 Book Robata no Ito Okashi 1 Month Ahead

Only 22 counter seats, reservation-only. Book via Instagram @robata.itookashi0411 at least 1 month before travel. Breakfast 8am–2pm (weekdays). Dinner 5pm–11pm. One of Fukuoka’s most talked-about dining experiences.

🚣 Takachiho Boat — Set an Alarm

Boat reservations open at exactly 9am JST, 14 days before your visit date. Set an alarm. Weekends sell out in minutes. Visit the official Takachiho Tourism website. Weekday visits (Thursday) recommended to avoid crowds. No phone reservations.

✈️ Ryokan Rooms Sell Out for Aug/Sep

Private open-air onsen rooms in Kurokawa and Yufuin sell out months ahead for peak summer/autumn. Book as soon as flights are confirmed — ideally by May/June — via Booking.com, Jalan, or Ikyu. These nights are the highlight of the trip.

🌋 Check Aso Crater Alert Levels

Check Japan Meteorological Agency (JMA) volcano alert levels 1–2 days before Day 5. At Level 2 or above, crater access closes. Have a backup plan (extend Kusasenri grasslands time). Most visits in late Aug/Sep proceed normally.

💴 Cash Is Essential

Yatai stalls, Tempura Hirao, many ryokan extras, and smaller restaurants are cash-only. Withdraw ¥80,000–100,000 at 7-Eleven or Japan Post ATMs at the airport (accepts Visa/Mastercard). Use YouTrip or Wise for the best SGD→JPY rate.

🚃 ETC Card for Expressway Tolls

Request an ETC card at the car rental counter (~¥550/day). Japan’s expressways have cashless ETC gates — the card handles everything automatically. Preload credit at rental office. Essential for the Aso, Takachiho, and Beppu drives.

🚂 JR Kyushu Northern Pass

3-Day Pass (¥4,000) covers Nagasaki Shinkansen, Beppu Sonic express, and the Yufuin no Mori scenic train. Buy online before travel on Klook or the JR Kyushu website. Must be activated on first day of use.

🌡️ Aug Heat Strategy

Fukuoka hits 30–33°C with high humidity in late August. Pack UV-protection shirts, a portable fan, and SPF 50+ sunscreen. Schedule outdoor activities before noon. Kakigori (shaved ice) and cold vending machine drinks are your friends.

📷 Route is Circular

The route starts near Fukuoka Airport (Day 1), loops south through Aso, Takachiho, Kurokawa, Yufuin, Beppu (Days 5–8), and returns to Fukuoka city for the final stretch (Days 9–12) — conveniently near the airport for Day 12 departure.
